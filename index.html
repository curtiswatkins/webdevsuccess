Steps to Success

These work. Take them with a grain of salt, because I myself am unsuccessful at them, but they do work.

1; Get in good shape

You must get in shape both physically and mentally. For me, this entails consistent exercise of both my body and spirit. I work out nearly every day and seek to stretch my mind and make time for God on a daily basis. This is crucial because it is was gives us the building blocks upon which we can become great men and women. Our healthy physical body allows us to shoulder the burdens of growth and success. Our healthy mind gives us the fortitude to confront challenges and the determination to finish difficult tasks.

2; Create goals

One of my least favorite questions to be asked is "What goals do you have?" or "What do you want out of life?". I dislike these questions because they are so difficult to answer and I feel that they always receive similar responses. Instead I would encourage you to ask yourself the following: "How do I want to impact the world?" or "What legacy do I want to leave behind?". While these questions are not much different they strike a different chord. They pull out what we want without specifically asking the question, "What do I want?"

Also, you should probably write these goals down somewhere you can see them or will review them often. I have been told that's important.

3; Accountability

A wise man once said: "Without accountability we will always be one step behind sucess, and two steps behind growth." - Me

One of the binding laws of nature is that everything natural moves towards disorder. This is known as entropy. In nature a system requires energy to return to a state of order, similarly, our goals and success require energy and accountability to maintain a state of order. Find someone to keep you accountable, whether it be a parent, spouse, friend, or boss. These people will aid you in consistently putting energy into your system. Keep the energy high and you will grow. Let it dissapate and your goals will become disorganized and your life will soon follow.

4; Find Someone to Serve

Some of the greatest instructions I have ever been given was to set goals for how I want to be able to serve people in the future. It is not instinctive to sacrifice for the benefit of others. However, service turns our personal growth from a linear progression to an exponential progression. I promise that as you do both small and large acts of service you will find much more than you ever have to give up in serving. I challenge you today to find one thing you can do every day to make another person's life better. It doesn't have to be big. Smile, open a door, do the dishes. Go make big changes in people lives by finding small ways to serve!

Why I do what I do

Throughout my many years of life I have found that I feel the most motivation and success when other people find success. I feel if I can help even just one person then I have done something great. Our lives aren't measured by the achievements we obtain but instead by the people who's lives we change.

Work Experience

I officiated soccer of all skill levels from the time I was 12 until I was 18. This gave me experience working with many differnet types of people, many of whom were angry because they believed I had made an incorrect call. This taught me resiliance and confidence.


Sport Court Surfacing

I finished and applied a surface to outdoor sport courts the summer of 2019. This was hard manual labor in the hot sun. It taught be hard work and persistance. I learned to be proud of what I had created yet humble and continually willing to learn how to improve.


Sales Associate at Al's Sporting Goods

This was a retail job that helped get me through my first year of college. I learned to follow instructions, work with a team, and be patient with people.

This was the most difficult job I have ever had. It was something that I didn't enjoy but I knew that I needed the financial freedom it would allow me during college. Conclusion

I know it's nothing special but I have found that often the most common of things bring us the most joy. It's not the fancy cars or the big paychecks, it's the smiles of other people as we look outside ourselves and serve. It's the confidence that comes from getting back up after we fall down. I am just an unsuccessful 21 year-old but I have learned a few things in my time. Most important is to never give up. I haven't done anything perfect the first time. I made mistakes and I got up again. I encourage you to look at your failures as stepping stones to create the life that you truly want. Now get out there and show me how to be succesful!!
